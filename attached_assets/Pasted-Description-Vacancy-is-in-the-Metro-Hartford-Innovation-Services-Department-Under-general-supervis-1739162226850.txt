Description
Vacancy is in the Metro Hartford Innovation Services Department. Under general supervision, ensures appropriate security controls are in place that will safeguard applications and platforms within the City of Hartford and Hartford Public Schools. Coordinates with end-users, engineers, application owners, and current Metro Hartford Innovation Services support staff to ensure the successful operations management of security processes. Implements, upgrades, and monitors security measures for the protection of the computer networks and information. Deploys, configures, and administers the security infrastructure and application. Plans and implements security measures to protect computer systems, networks and data. Assists staff with designing and implementing safeguards to protect computer and telecommunication systems. Manages and monitors intrusion detection and prevention systems. Installs and uses software, such as firewalls and data encryption programs, to protect sensitive information. Investigates suspected and actual breaches of security and undertake reporting/remedial action as required. Establishes system controls by developing framework for controls and levels of access; recommends improvements. Conducts periodic vulnerability and security audits. Evaluates Data Loss Prevention use cases across Data-in-Motion, Data-in-Use, and Data-at-Rest. Develops and implements policies related to secure hardware and software. Provides after-hours network support on a periodic on-call basis. Performs related work as required.

This is a HMEA union position. The hours of this position are 40 per week and the salary includes 5% in lieu of overtime.
Knowledge, Skills & Abilities
The examination will consist of a rating of your training and experience as contained on your application and may include a written test, an oral test, a performance test, or a combination thereof. All parts of the examination, including tests and ratings, will be related to the requirements of the position. The examination will be designed to measure the following:

Knowledge of:
Troubleshooting firewalls, Intrusion Detection, VPN appliances, vulnerability assessment tools, event and log analysis, security change tracking and other network security systems and devices
Center for Internet Security (CIS), National Institute for Standards and Technology (NIST), and Information Technology Infrastructure Library (ITIL)
Data communications and networking
Firewalls, proxies, SIEM, antivirus, and IDPS concepts
Malware distribution and technical risk
Networking protocols and theory
Skills in:
Patch management with the ability to deploy patches in a timely manner while understanding business impact
Analytics and problem-solving with a results-oriented focus
Project management and organization
Ability to:
Devise and enforce Network and OS security policies while balancing the needs of a diverse and dynamic business
Write scripts at a high level
Identify and mitigate network vulnerabilities and explain how to avoid them
Analyze overall network security posture as well as specific events
Understand and apply new processes, procedures and software tools
Work in a fast paced and flexible environment, particularly on critical care and on-call duties
Establish and maintain effective working relationships with stakeholders
Physical Demands and Working Conditions
Work is performed primarily in an office environment with some travel to different sites within the City or Hartford Public Schools. Incumbent may be required to work extended hours including evenings and weekends.
